Vesicular transport routes and membrane contact site tethering architecture

Organelles & Trafficking Routes
ER — Protein synthesis, lipid synthesis, Ca2+ storage
Golgi — Protein processing, lipid remodeling, sorting
Endosome — Cargo sorting, recycling decisions
Mitochondria — ATP synthesis, Ca2+ homeostasis, ROS
Lysosome — Degradation, autophagy, nutrient sensing
PM — Exocytosis, endocytosis, signaling
Membrane Contact Site (MCS) Architecture & Function
ER-Mitochondria (MAMs): VAPB-PTPIP51 tethering, MFN2 fusion proteins, IP3R-VDAC coupling. Function: Ca2+ transfer, lipid exchange (PS, PE), metabolic feedback, apoptosis signaling.
ER-Endosome: STARD3/StARD3 (oxysterol binding), ORP1L (KIF5 recruitment). Cholesterol transfer via lipid-binding pockets. Critical for lysosomal/endosomal function & Ca2+ dynamics.
Lysosome-Mitochondria: Rab7-dependent tethering. mTORC1 translocates to lysosomes to sense amino acids, activates TFEB. Nutrient availability signal integration.
ER-Lysosome: ORP1L/STARD3 cholesterol exchange. Lysosomes serve as cholesterol hub. Important for sphingolipid homeostasis. Links ER lipid supply to lysosomal degradation capacity.
