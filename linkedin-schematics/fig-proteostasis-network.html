Cellular Proteostasis Network

Interconnected quality control systems maintaining protein homeostasis under cellular stress

Quality Control Systems
Chaperones — ATP-dependent protein folding assistance
Proteasome System — K48-Ub chain recognition & degradation
Autophagy — Cargo sequestration & lysosomal digestion
ER-QC — ERAD & UPR signaling coordination
Aggresome — Pericentrosomal inclusion deposition
Stress — Protein misfolding, oxidative, energy stress
Detailed System Descriptions
Chaperone System: HSP70 (cytoplasmic & ER), HSP90 (client-dependent), HSP60 (mitochondrial), sHSPs (holdases). Refold or mark for degradation via ubiquitination.
UPS: E1 ubiquitin activases, E2 conjugases, E3 ligases (RING, HECT, RBR) add K48/K11 chains. 26S proteasome (~2.5 MDa) catalyzes ATP-dependent proteolysis.
Autophagy: Selective autophagy receptors (p62, NBR1, OPTN, CALCOCO2) recognize K63/K48 ubiquitinated substrates. Bulk autophagy during nutrient/energy stress. Lysosomal hydrolases complete degradation.
ER-QC: BiP/GRP78 senses ER stress, activates IRE1α (splices XBP1s), PERK (phosphorylates eIF2α), ATF6 (N-terminal cleavage). ERAD extracts misfolded ER proteins to cytoplasm for proteasomal degradation.
Aggresome: When UPS saturated, HDAC6 recognizes K63-linked ubiquitin chains via its ubiquitin-binding domain (UBD), recruits dynein motor for retrograde transport to MTOC. Creates perinuclear inclusion.
